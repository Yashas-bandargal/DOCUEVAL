Data Security Policy

1. Overview
This policy defines the standards for protecting organizational data from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. All employees are responsible for understanding and following this policy.

2. Data Classification
Data is classified into three levels:
- Confidential: Includes financial records, HR data, legal documents, and trade secrets. Access is strictly limited.
- Internal: Includes internal memos, project plans, and operational data. Accessible to all employees.
- Public: Includes marketing materials and press releases. No restrictions.

3. Access Control
Access to confidential data is granted on a need-to-know basis. All access requests must be approved by the data owner and the IT security team. Access rights must be reviewed every 6 months.

4. Password Policy
All system passwords must be at least 12 characters long and include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters. Passwords must be changed every 90 days. Reuse of the last 5 passwords is prohibited.

5. Encryption
All confidential data must be encrypted at rest using AES-256 and in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher. Employees must not transmit confidential data via unencrypted email.

6. Incident Reporting
Any suspected data breach or security incident must be reported immediately to the IT security team at security@company.com. The IT team will assess and respond within 24 hours.

7. Third-Party Vendors
Vendors with access to organizational data must sign a Data Processing Agreement. The IT team must conduct a security assessment of all new vendors before data sharing begins.

8. Consequences of Non-Compliance
Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, and may involve legal proceedings in serious cases.
